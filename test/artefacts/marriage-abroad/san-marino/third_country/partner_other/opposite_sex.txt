Marriage in San Marino

Contact the relevant local authorities in San Marino to find out about local marriage laws, including what documents you’ll need.

^You should [get legal advice](/government/collections/list-of-lawyers) and check the [travel advice for San Marino](/foreign-travel-advice/san-marino) before making any plans.

There aren’t any British consular facilities to register a marriage or get legal documents to prove you’re eligible to marry in San Marino.

You normally have to pay fees for consular services in the local currency - these are shown in the list of [consular fees for San Marino](/government/publications/san-marino-consular-fees).

You can pay by cash or credit card, but not by personal cheque.

##Naturalisation of your partner if they move to the UK

Your partner can apply to [become a British citizen](/becoming-a-british-citizen/if-your-spouse-is-a-british-citizen) once they’ve lived in the UK for 3 years.
